GST COMPLIANCE FOR TECHNOLOGY COMPANIES - INDIA M&A DUE DILIGENCE

KEY AREAS TO VERIFY:

1. REGISTRATION STATUS
- Mandatory GST registration if annual turnover exceeds INR 20 lakhs
- For digital services: registration required regardless of turnover
- Penalty for non-registration: 10% of tax due, minimum INR 10,000

2. FILING COMPLIANCE
- GSTR-1: Monthly/quarterly return for outward supplies
- GSTR-3B: Monthly summary return
- GSTR-9: Annual return
- Non-filing penalty: INR 50 per day (INR 20 per day for nil returns)

3. INPUT TAX CREDIT
- ITC can be claimed on software, cloud services, office equipment
- Blocked credits: personal expenses, motor vehicles
- Reversal required if payment not made within 180 days

4. M&A DUE DILIGENCE CHECKLIST
- GST registration certificate valid? YES/NO
- All returns filed for last 3 years? YES/NO
- Any pending GST notices or demands? YES/NO
- ITC reconciliation done? YES/NO
- E-invoicing implemented (if applicable)? YES/NO

5. COMMON ISSUES IN TECH STARTUPS
- Incorrect classification of software services
- Missing GST on imports of services (reverse charge)
- ITC claimed on personal expenses
- Late filing leading to interest @ 18% per annum

6. PENALTY EXPOSURE IN M&A
- Undisclosed GST liability transfers to acquiring company
- Average undisclosed liability in tech acquisitions: INR 20-200 lakhs
- Always obtain GST clearance certificate before deal closure
